Local Business of the Month

Kalinaw Coffee House, Vigan

Inside a 1920s bahay-na-bato on Calle Crisologo, third-generation owner Nena Reyes still roasts her beans the way her grandfather did — in small batches, over charcoal, timed by ear rather than a dial. The café survives on regulars who’ve been coming since before the tiles cracked.

What keeps Kalinaw going isn’t nostalgia marketing — it’s that nothing about it has been staged for a feed. The heritage is load-bearing, not decorative.

Creator Spotlight

The Weaver Reviving Ifugao’s Lost Patterns

Maria Bugan spent a decade tracing patterns that had all but disappeared from Ifugao weaving, cross-referencing faded textiles in provincial museums with the memory of elders who could still name them by heart.

Today her loom produces pieces that museums now borrow back — a quiet reversal of the usual direction culture tends to travel in.

The Brand

What is Overview PH?

Overview PH began as a simple question: what happens if a travel story slows down long enough to actually look? Not just at the view, but at the hands that built it, the recipe that survived three generations, the barangay that shows up at 5 a.m. to keep a tradition alive.

We document the Philippines the way a good neighbor would tell you about it — with context, with names, with the parts that don’t make it onto a postcard. Every story is reported on the ground, every café visited in person, every creator interviewed face to face.

This isn’t a listicle factory. It’s a running record of a country still being discovered by the people who live in it.
